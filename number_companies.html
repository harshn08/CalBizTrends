A lot has been changing in the industry. While the space is opening up for entrepreneurs to launch new ideas, companies with expertise in computer science are still the most sought after initiatives to start a new company. According to Wired Academic, the IT sector is predicted to grow by 22 percent in the next seven years. However, even with this projection, it is surprising to know that the biotech industry has been the fastest emerging sector after IT. The state accounts for 47 percent of national R&D spending on biotechnology and generates 53 percent of the nation's biotech revenues. California's strength in research, as well as its large share of the U.S. biotech industry, will continue to make it one of the most attractive places to form biotech companies. It is closely being followed by the mobile industry in third place in terms of new companies started.
